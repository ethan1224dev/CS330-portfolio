# CS330-portfolio

# How do I approach designing software?

I approach designing software by first figuring out what the final program needs to do before writing code. Breaking the project into smaller parts makes it easier to stay organized and solve one problem at a time. For this project, I planned the objects I wanted in my 3D scene first, then decided how each one could be created from simple shapes before adding textures and lighting.

# What new design skills has your work on the project helped you to craft?

This project helped me improve my ability to build more complex objects from basic geometric shapes instead of relying on pre-made models. I also learned how to think more carefully about object placement, scaling, lighting, and textures so the overall scene looked more realistic.

# What design process did you follow for your project work?

I started by deciding on a simple desk setup that I could realistically complete within the project requirements. I created the larger objects first, such as the desk and laptop, then added smaller details like the coffee mug, pencil holder, and pencils within the pencil holder. Once the scene was complete, I focused on lighting, textures, and animation to improve the final presentation.

# How could tactics from your design approach be applied in future work?

Breaking a project into smaller pieces makes large assignments much easier to manage. Planning before coding and building features one at a time also makes it easier to find mistakes and make improvements. I can apply this same process to future software development projects.

# How do I approach developing programs?

I develop programs by building one feature at a time and testing it before moving on to the next step. Working incrementally helps prevent small problems from turning into larger ones and makes debugging much easier.

# What new development strategies did you use while working on your 3D scene?

One strategy I used was reusing functions and code whenever possible instead of rewriting similar sections. I also spent more time testing changes after each milestone instead of waiting until the very end. This helped me catch issues earlier and made development more manageable.

# How did iteration factor into your development?

Iteration was an important part of this project. I continuously adjusted object positions, sizes, textures, and lighting until the scene looked the way I wanted. Each revision improved the overall quality of the final project.

# How has your approach to developing code evolved throughout the milestones, which led you to the project's completion?

Throughout the milestones, I became more comfortable working with OpenGL and understanding how the different parts of the graphics pipeline worked together. Instead of trying to complete everything at once, I learned to focus on one problem at a time and gradually build toward the finished project.

# How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?

Computer science provides the problem-solving and programming skills. Projects like this strengthen my ability to design, develop, debug, and improve software, which are valuable skills in many areas of programming.

# How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills that can be applied in your future educational pathway?

Computational graphics helped me better understand concepts such as transformations, lighting, textures, cameras, and rendering. These topics build a stronger foundation for future computer science courses involving graphics, game development, simulation, and visualization.

# How do computational graphics and visualizations give you new knowledge and skills that can be applied in your future professional pathway?

Learning computational graphics has given me practical experience working with debugging visual applications and creating interactive programs. Even if I do not work directly in graphics programming, these experiences improve my overall programming, problem-solving, and software development skills, which will be useful throughout my professional career.
